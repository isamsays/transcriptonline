Steps to Obtain a University Degree and Transcripts from the UK

1. Contact Your University's Records Office

The first step is to reach out to your university's records or student services office. They can provide:

  • Replacement degree certificates

  • Official academic transcripts

  • Certified copies of your documents

Most universities offer online request forms for alumni and current students.

2. Verification and Authentication

To ensure your documents are accepted internationally, you may need to have them verified and authenticated. This process typically involves:

  • Notarisation by a UK solicitor or notary public

  • Verification with the issuing university (sometimes requiring your written consent)

  • Certification that the documents are genuine

3. Apostille Legalisation

For use in countries that are part of the Hague Convention, your degree and transcripts must be apostilled by the UK Foreign, Commonwealth & Development Office (FCDO). The apostille certifies the authenticity of the signature and seal on your documents.

4. Embassy Attestation (If Needed)

If you plan to use your documents in a country not part of the Hague Convention, you may need additional attestation at the relevant embassy in London. This step ensures your documents are recognized in your destination country.
